10 Ways to Travel More Sustainably

By Maria Rodriguez August 28, 2025

Sustainable travel doesn't mean sacrificing comfort or adventure. With thoughtful planning and conscious choices, you can reduce your environmental impact while still having incredible travel experiences. Here are ten practical ways to make your journeys more eco-friendly.

1. Choose Direct Flights When Possible

Takeoffs and landings consume the most fuel, so direct flights have a lower carbon footprint per mile than connecting flights. When direct flights aren't available, consider overland transportation for shorter distances.

2. Pack Light and Smart

Every extra pound increases fuel consumption. Pack versatile clothing that can be mixed and matched, and stick to carry-on luggage when possible. This also saves you baggage fees and reduces the risk of lost luggage.

3. Stay in Eco-Certified Accommodations

Look for hotels and lodges with green certifications like LEED, Green Key, or local eco-labels. These properties implement energy-saving practices, water conservation, and waste reduction programs.

4. Use Public Transportation

Trains, buses, and local public transit have a much lower carbon footprint per passenger than private vehicles or taxis. Plus, using public transport often provides a more authentic local experience.

5. Eat Local and Seasonal

Choose restaurants that serve locally-sourced, seasonal ingredients. This reduces transportation emissions and supports local farmers and communities. Try traditional dishes made with regional ingredients.

6. Bring Reusable Items

Pack a reusable water bottle, shopping bag, utensils, and food containers. This reduces single-use plastic waste and saves money on purchasing disposable items during your trip.

7. Respect Wildlife and Natural Areas

Follow "Leave No Trace" principles: stay on marked trails, don't disturb wildlife, and pack out all trash. Choose tour operators that prioritize conservation and animal welfare.

8. Support Local Communities

Buy from local artisans, eat at family-owned restaurants, and choose locally-owned accommodations. This ensures your tourism dollars directly benefit the communities you visit.

9. Travel Slowly

Instead of rushing through multiple destinations, spend more time in fewer places. This reduces transportation emissions and allows for deeper cultural immersion and more meaningful experiences.

10. Offset Your Carbon Footprint

Calculate your trip's carbon emissions and purchase verified carbon offsets through reputable programs. While this shouldn't be your only sustainability effort, it can help neutralize unavoidable emissions.

Making Sustainable Choices

Sustainable travel is about making conscious choices that minimize negative impacts while maximizing positive ones. Every small action adds up, and as more travelers adopt these practices, the tourism industry continues to evolve toward greater sustainability.

Remember, the goal isn't perfection – it's progress. Start with the changes that feel most manageable for you, and gradually incorporate more sustainable practices into your travel routine.
